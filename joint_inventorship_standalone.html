Joint inventorship self-assessment
A guided interview for researchers and academics
Purpose
This tool helps you work through whether your contribution to a research project may support a claim for joint inventorship on a patent. It asks a series of factual questions about the nature, origin, and timing of your contribution, and guides you toward a preliminary assessment of whether the legal threshold has been met.
The threshold this interview focuses on is conception of the idea for an invention
What is conception?
Conception is the point at which someone has a clear and specific idea of how an invention would work — not just a goal or direction, but an actual solution. In joint inventorship, you do not need to have come up with the entire invention yourself. Contributing a specific idea to even one part of the invention can be enough. Conception is the starting point for any inventorship claim and is the main focus of this interview.

It is worth knowing that being named an inventor on a patent is different from being an author on a paper. Academic authorship can reflect many kinds of contributions — supervision, guidance, data collection, writing. Patent inventorship is a narrower legal standard that focuses specifically on who contributed the ideas that make the invention protectable. The two do not always overlap.
Who this is for
Graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, faculty, and other academics who contributed to a research project and believe they may have a claim to joint inventorship — whether or not a patent has been filed yet. You do not need legal knowledge to use this tool. The questions are framed around things you experienced and can describe in your own words.
What it will ask
Questions about what kind of contribution you made, when you made it, where the idea came from, and how it relates to the invention. Most people complete the interview in five to ten minutes, depending on how many follow-up questions apply to their situation. You can go back at any point to revise a response.
What it will not assess
This interview only addresses the threshold question of conception. A patent attorney will assess everything else necessary to evaluate the full merits of your claim.
Important — please read before proceeding
This tool does not constitute legal advice and does not create an attorney-client relationship. It is designed to help you organize your thinking before consulting a professional — not to replace that consultation.
If you are affiliated with a university or research institution, your institution may have IP policies that affect your rights, including obligations to disclose and assign inventions. This tool does not account for those policies. You should consult your institution's technology transfer office or an attorney before taking any action.
